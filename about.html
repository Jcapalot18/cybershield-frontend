Why I built this

I've always been drawn to cybersecurity — following outlets like CyberWire, reading about breaches, watching how attackers operate. What I kept seeing was a pattern: small businesses were getting destroyed while the tools to protect them were priced for Fortune 500 companies.

A dental office, a law firm, an accounting practice — these businesses handle some of the most sensitive data imaginable. Patient records. Financial information. Confidential legal documents. But they have no IT team, no security budget, and no way to know if they've already been compromised.

I decided to build the solution. Not because it was easy — it wasn't — but because the problem was real and nobody was solving it at the right price point for small businesses.

What drives me

Since I was young I wanted to build something that actually mattered. Not just a successful business in terms of revenue — but a business that's successful because of what it does for people.

The world is advancing at a rapid pace. Cyber threats are evolving faster than most small businesses can keep up with. I built CyberShield to close that gap — to give a Brooklyn law firm the same level of protection that a major corporation has, for $97 a month.

That's the mission. That's what gets me up every day.
