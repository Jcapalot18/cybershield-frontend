Cookie Policy

Last updated: June 1, 2026 · Effective: June 1, 2026
The short version: CyberShield uses minimal cookies. We don't use advertising cookies or tracking pixels. We only use what's necessary to keep you logged in and make the platform work.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit a website. They help websites remember information about your visit, like your login status or preferences.

2. How CyberShield Uses Cookies

CyberShield uses sessionStorage (not traditional cookies) for authentication. This means your login session is stored temporarily in your browser and is automatically cleared when you close the tab.

We do not use:

  • Advertising or retargeting cookies
  • Third-party tracking cookies
  • Analytics cookies that identify you personally
  • Cross-site tracking of any kind

4. Third-Party Services

CyberShield uses a small number of third-party services that may set their own cookies:

  • Stripe — for payment processing. Stripe may set cookies to prevent fraud. See Stripe's Privacy Policy.
  • Google Fonts — for typography. Google may log requests. See Google's Privacy Policy.

5. Your Choices

Since we use sessionStorage rather than cookies, standard browser cookie controls don't apply. Your session data is automatically cleared when you close your browser tab.

You can also manually clear your session data at any time by going to your browser's Developer Tools → Application → Session Storage and clearing the CyberShield entries.

6. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time. We'll notify you by email if we make material changes.
